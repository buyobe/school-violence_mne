Why This Matters for the NGO

These findings highlight priority areas for intervention. High-case regions and districts should be targeted for awareness campaigns, while schools with persistent reports may need direct support such as teacher training, counseling services, or stronger reporting mechanisms.

Policy Impact

The data collected aligns with national frameworks such as the Law of the Child Act (2009), the National Plan of Action to End Violence Against Women and Children (2017–2022), and Tanzania’s commitments under the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG 16.2) to end abuse, exploitation, trafficking, and all forms of violence against children.

By identifying regions and schools with the highest cases, NGOs can provide evidence to government stakeholders and advocate for stronger enforcement of these policies, improved child protection services, and increased funding for prevention programs.

  • Collaborate with local government to enforce child protection policies and align with Tanzania’s National Plan of Action.
